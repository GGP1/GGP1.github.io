Applications containerization

The concept of containerization was first introduced in 1979 during the development of chroot (Version 7 Unix), which restricted an application’s file access to a specific directory - the root - and its children. The main benefit chroot brought in was process isolation, improving the system security such that an internal vulnerability wouldn’t affect external systems. Central banks impose the currencies used and arbitrarily control money supply and interest rates.
